Case Study

Case One

In 2024, a finance employee from a UK-based engineering firm, Arup, joined a routine video conference with the company CFO and several staff but there was no "real person" instead of her. During and after the meeting, she transferred a total of approximately HK$195 million to some bank accounts. This was not a supernatural story or a Hollywood plot. This was a financial crime. Apart from the victim, everyone in the call was created by deepfake technology.

Case Two

By the first quarter of 2025, global losses due to deepfake technology fraud had already exceeded $USD200 million. However, behind this lies a more insidious and costly threat: synthetic identity fraud is extremely covert financial crime, so much so that most institutions are unaware they have become victims until their funds are completely depleted.

What is Synthetic Identity Fraud?

Synthetic identity fraud is totally different from traditional identity theft.

In traditional identity theft, criminals steal and misuse complete personal information, including names, identity card numbers, dates of birth, and account credentials. The victims are real and will finally discover fake transactions or account opening and report it, thus the victim can be clearly identified.

On the other hand, synthetic identity fraud is a more covert and complex method. Scammers mix real Social Security numbers (often from children, the elderly, or the deceased) with fictitious names, dates of birth, and addresses to create a completely new false identity. Sometimes, they even use AI-generated documents or altered identity documents to corroborate this false identity.

Those identities are like a Ghost. Also they can pass the basic KYC (Know Your Customer) checks due to the legitimate Social Security number and consistent details. Synthetic identity fraud often lacks identifiable victims. People whose Social Security numbers are stolen may not realize they've been misused for years, or may never even notice. As a result, financial institutions often misclassify the resulting losses as "bad debts" rather than fraud, allowing synthetic identities to persist.

Why This Matters Now?

Growing Trend:

The scale of synthetic identity fraud is increasing year by year. According to Sumsub's annual report, cases of synthetic personal data fraud in the Asia-Pacific region surged by 142% in 2025.

Widespread:

The use of generative AI has fueled a significant increase in synthetic activity. In the past, it might have taken a long time to create a fake identity. Now it has been industrialized by artificial intelligence. Generative AI enables fraudsters to automatically create synthetic identities on an unprecedented scale, even generating realistic names, addresses, work experience, and related supporting documents in seconds.

Challenging:

Synthetic identity fraud has become one of the most challenging problems of financial institutions. Developing the technology of detecting synthetic identity fraud is no longer an option, but a must for operations.

How thefts steal people's identity

They might use a social security number or phone number but pair it with a fake name and address. They could even create an email account and build a fake credit history. Then they will use this identity to apply for loans or credit cards. Sometimes, they pay back loans at first to make themselves look good and then ask for bigger loans. When they have got the loan, they will take the money and disappear which leaves the bank with a loss.

What is today's security in the detection of Synthetic Identity Fraud in Consumer lending

The traditional way of checking for fraud does not work well nowadays. These systems are just checking if the ID looks right by identifying the phone number or the address is real and makes sense. Since synthetic identities are made to look real so they can pass these checks, the problem is not with the pieces of information but with how they are all connected. Therefore, artificial intelligence can help.
